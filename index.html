Welcome

Social media algorithms shape what users watch, read, and believe every day. Platforms like TikTok, Instagram, YouTube, and Facebook use data-driven systems to personalize feeds and maximize engagement.

This project explains how algorithms work, their benefits, ethical concerns, and recommendations for improving transparency and user safety.

How Social Media Algorithms Work

Social media algorithms decide what shows up on your feed. These platforms do not simply show random posts. They study user behavior and use collected information to predict what content will keep users interested.

Examples of what algorithms track:

  • Likes
  • Comments
  • Shares
  • Watch time
  • Searches
  • Accounts followed

The Feed Process

User Activity
↓
Platform Collects Data
↓
Algorithm Makes Predictions
↓
Posts Get Ranked
↓
Personalized Feed

Example: If you watch gym videos often, your feed may begin showing workout clips, fitness influencers, diet tips, and gym-related products.

Why It Matters

Helpful Side

  • Shows users content they enjoy
  • Helps people discover creators
  • Supports businesses and advertisers
  • Makes apps easier to use

Harmful Side

  • Can create filter bubbles
  • Can spread misinformation
  • Can push addictive content
  • Can affect privacy and mental health

Quick Recommendations

Control

Users should have chronological feed options and reset tools.

Transparency

Platforms should explain why posts are recommended.

Safety

Reduce harmful content loops, especially for minors.

Accountability

Companies should better manage misinformation.
