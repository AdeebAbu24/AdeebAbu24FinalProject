Welcome

Social media algorithms shape what billions of users watch, read, and believe every day. Platforms like TikTok, Instagram, YouTube, and Facebook use data-driven systems to personalize feeds, maximize engagement, and influence digital experiences.

This project explores how social media algorithms work, their benefits, ethical concerns, privacy risks, social impact, and recommendations for making them safer and more transparent.

How Social Media Algorithms Work

Social media algorithms decide what shows up on your feed. These platforms study user behavior and use collected information to predict what content will keep users interested the longest.

Examples of what algorithms track:

  • Likes
  • Comments
  • Shares
  • Watch time
  • Searches
  • Accounts followed

The Feed Process

User Activity
↓
Platform Collects Data
↓
Algorithm Makes Predictions
↓
Posts Get Ranked
↓
Personalized Feed

Example: If you watch gym videos often, your feed may begin showing workout clips, fitness influencers, diet tips, and gym-related products.

Why It Matters

Helpful Side

  • Shows users content they enjoy
  • Helps people discover creators
  • Supports businesses and advertisers
  • Makes apps easier to use

Harmful Side

  • Can create filter bubbles
  • Can spread misinformation
  • Can push addictive content
  • Can affect privacy and mental health
