Recommendations for Social Media Algorithms

Making Algorithms Safer, More Transparent, and Easier for Users to Control

Home Technology Benefits & Risks Ethics Social Issues Recommendations

Introduction

Social media algorithms help users find content, creators, and communities that match their interests. However, they can also create problems when users are repeatedly shown harmful content, misinformation, or posts chosen only to keep them engaged.

Because algorithms shape what people see online, platforms should make them safer, more transparent, and easier for users to control.

Main Recommendations

Chronological Feed Option

Users should be able to switch to a chronological feed. This would let them see posts in the order they were posted instead of only seeing what the algorithm chooses.

Explain Why Posts Are Recommended

Platforms should clearly explain why certain posts appear on a user's feed. For example, the app could say, “You are seeing this because you watched similar videos.”

Let Users Adjust Their Feed

Users should have the ability to reset, adjust, or remove topics from their feed. This would help people avoid content that feels repetitive, negative, or distracting.

Limit Harmful Content Loops

Platforms should reduce repeated harmful content, especially for younger users. This includes content connected to self-harm, eating disorders, violence, or extreme misinformation.

Reducing Misinformation

Control False Information Better

Social media companies should limit misinformation during major events such as elections, health emergencies, and breaking news.

Promote Reliable Sources

Platforms should make trusted sources easier to find when users search for serious topics like politics, public health, or major world events.

Label Misleading Content

Posts with false or misleading information should be clearly labeled so users know when content may not be reliable.

Allow Outside Research

Platforms should allow researchers to study how algorithms affect misinformation, mental health, privacy, and public opinion.

Balanced Recommendation

The goal is not to remove social media algorithms completely. Instead, platforms should improve them so they protect users while still helping people find useful and interesting content.

Key Takeaway

Social media algorithms should support users, not trap them in harmful or misleading feeds. Giving users more control, improving transparency, reducing misinformation, and limiting harmful content loops can make these systems safer and more responsible.
