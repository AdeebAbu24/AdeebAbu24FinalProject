Recommendations

How social media platforms can make algorithms safer, fairer, and easier for users to control.

Overview Recommendations Main Goal

Why Recommendations Matter

Social media algorithms can be useful because they help users find content they enjoy. However, they can also create problems when users are stuck with algorithm picks, shown repeated harmful content, or exposed to misinformation. Because of this, platforms should give users more control and make recommendation systems more transparent.

Ways to Make Algorithms Less Harmful

1

Chronological Feed Option

Users should be able to switch to a chronological feed so they can see posts in the order they were posted, instead of only seeing what the algorithm chooses for them.

2

Explain Recommendations

Platforms should clearly show why a post is recommended. For example, the app could say, “You are seeing this because you watched similar videos.”

3

Let Users Change Their Feed

Users should have tools to adjust, reset, or remove certain topics from their feed. This would help people avoid content that feels repetitive, negative, or distracting.

4

Limit Repeating Harmful Content

Platforms should reduce harmful content loops, especially for younger users. This includes repeated content about self-harm, eating disorders, violence, or extreme ideas.

5

Control Misinformation Better

Social media companies should do a better job controlling misinformation during major events such as elections, health emergencies, and breaking news. False information can spread quickly when algorithms push content that gets strong reactions.

Main Goal

The goal is not to remove social media algorithms completely. Instead, platforms should build systems that protect users while still helping them find useful and interesting content. Algorithms should support people, not trap them in harmful or misleading feeds.
